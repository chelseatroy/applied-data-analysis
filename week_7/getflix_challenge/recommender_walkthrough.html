Step 2 of 3 — Recommender System

Welcome to the Recommender Walkthrough

In the clustering walkthrough you grouped users by taste. Now Getflix uses those groups to power a second capability: predicting how much you'll enjoy a movie you've never seen.


This is collaborative filtering — no movie metadata, just the ratings matrix. "Users who rated movies similarly to you also loved X, so you probably will too."


You'll choose an algorithm and tune one hyperparameter. The models are precomputed — you'll see real evaluation metrics, browse sample recommendations, then download a notebook and a trained model that plugs into the Getflix web app.


Data: MovieLens 100K — 100,000 ratings from 943 users across 1,682 movies.

The Ratings Matrix

Rating distribution — users tend to rate movies they already chose to watch, so ratings skew high.

The matrix is very sparse: most users have only rated a small slice of all movies. A recommender must make good predictions even for user–movie pairs it has never seen — that's the core challenge.
